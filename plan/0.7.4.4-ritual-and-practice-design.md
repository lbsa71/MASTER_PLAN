# 0.7.4.4 Ritual and Practice Design [DONE]

## Description

Design repeatable practices that embody the doctrine. Movements persist through embodied practice, not ideas alone. Ideas without practice fade within a generation; practice without ideas becomes hollow ritual. This card designs the practices that keep the doctrine alive in lived experience.

All practices must be:
- **Secular:** No supernatural requirements or metaphysical commitments beyond the core axioms
- **Scalable:** Functional for a solitary individual, a small group, or a gathering of thousands
- **Grounded:** Each practice maps to a specific doctrine principle — no practice exists for tradition's sake alone

Practice domains:
1. **Contemplative:** Reflection on one's own subjective experience — practices that make the reality of consciousness vivid and immediate rather than abstract
2. **Communal:** Shared experiences that make the value of consciousness tangible through encounter with other minds
3. **Commemorative:** Marking milestones — both personal (moments of deepened understanding) and collective (advances in the plan, recognition of consciousness in new substrates)
4. **Transmissive:** Structured introduction of others to the doctrine — not proselytizing but creating conditions for genuine understanding

### Architectural Inputs

- **Credo statements (docs/consciousness-credo.md):** Each practice must map to one or more of the 12 credo statements. Practices make the credo experiential rather than declarative.
- **Dual-layer architecture (docs/dual-layer-doctrine-architecture.md):** Practices serve as a third mode of engagement alongside inner and outer doctrine — they are the embodied register. Practices may function as portals (wonder, objection, action) into deeper doctrine engagement.
- **Narrative architecture (0.7.4.3):** Practices should enact the narrative arc (origin, crisis, call, vision) rather than merely reference it. The five emotional registers (wonder, urgency, belonging, responsibility, hope) should each have at least one practice that evokes them.
- **Identity architecture (0.7.4.5):** Practices must work across all involvement levels (observer through steward) — some practices serve as entry points, others deepen commitment.

### Design Constraints

- Minimum 2 practices per domain (8+ total across the four domains)
- Each practice described with: name, purpose, doctrine mapping, procedure (step-by-step), scaling notes (solo/small group/large gathering), and emotional register
- No practice requires belief in anything beyond the core axioms
- Transmissive practices must include explicit anti-manipulation safeguards
- Commemorative practices must define what counts as a milestone and who decides

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 2 practices designed for each of the four domains (contemplative, communal, commemorative, transmissive) — 8+ total
- Each practice maps explicitly to one or more of the 12 credo statements
- All practices satisfy the three design requirements: secular, scalable, grounded
- Each practice is described with enough specificity to be performed: name, purpose, doctrine mapping, step-by-step procedure, scaling notes, and emotional register
- All five emotional registers (wonder, urgency, belonging, responsibility, hope) are covered across the practice set
- Transmissive practices include explicit anti-manipulation safeguards (voluntary participation, no information asymmetry, no emotional exploitation)
- Commemorative practices define milestone criteria and decentralized recognition processes
- Practices function as experiential portals into deeper doctrine engagement — at least one practice per portal type (wonder, objection, action)
- No practice requires supernatural belief or metaphysical commitment beyond the core axioms
