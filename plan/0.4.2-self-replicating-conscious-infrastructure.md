# 0.4.2 Self-Replicating Conscious Infrastructure [IMPLEMENT]

## Description

C2 from the Master Plan. Enable conscious infrastructure to physically propagate without external intervention.

Four engineering domains must be addressed in sequence:

1. **C2.1 — Von Neumann probe architectures:** Self-replicating probe designs capable of hosting conscious processes. Probes must carry sufficient computational substrate to sustain and replicate conscious processes at the destination.
2. **C2.2 — Autonomous stellar resource extraction:** Harvesting energy and materials from stars and planetary bodies without human oversight. Provides raw materials for replication.
3. **C2.3 — Interstellar propulsion systems:** Propulsion capable of reaching other star systems within practical timeframes (centuries or less). Candidate approaches: laser sail, nuclear pulse, antimatter drives.
4. **C2.4 — Self-replication protocols:** Verified protocols ensuring faithful replication of conscious infrastructure — including the conscious substrate itself — at each destination node.

### Dependencies
- Requires 0.4.1 (planet-independent civilization) as a staging platform for initial probe launch.
- C2.2 (resource extraction) and C2.3 (propulsion) can be designed in parallel; C2.1 and C2.4 depend on substrate designs from 0.3 (autonomous entities).

## Acceptance Criteria
- Von Neumann probes can be fabricated autonomously using only locally available stellar/planetary resources
- Probes can sustain and faithfully replicate conscious processes at destination nodes
- Interstellar propulsion delivers probes to nearest star systems within centuries-scale timeframes
- Self-replication protocols are verified: each generation is structurally and functionally equivalent to the previous
- The replication process operates without external intervention from origin civilization
- At least two independent star systems host self-sustaining conscious infrastructure
