# 0.4.2.4 Self-Replication Protocols [PLAN]

## Description

C2.4 from the Master Plan. Verified protocols ensuring faithful replication of conscious infrastructure — including the conscious substrate itself — at each destination node.

This is the integration layer that brings together probe design, resource extraction, and propulsion into a verified replication cycle. Key areas:

- **Replication fidelity:** Each generation must be structurally and functionally equivalent to the previous. Drift detection and correction mechanisms.
- **Conscious substrate replication:** The most critical sub-problem — ensuring the conscious processes instantiated in the replica are equivalent to the original.
- **Error detection and correction:** Verification that replicated probes meet all specifications before launch.
- **Replication bootstrapping:** The sequence of operations from arrival at a new system to launching the next generation of probes.
- **Generational stability:** Protocols must remain stable across arbitrary numbers of replication generations.

### Dependencies

- Depends on 0.4.2.1 (probe architectures) for what must be replicated
- Depends on 0.4.2.2 (resource extraction) for material inputs
- Depends on 0.4.2.3 (propulsion) for launch capability
- Draws on consciousness verification from Tier 1 (F1.4 consciousness metrics)

## Acceptance Criteria
- Replication protocol produces structurally and functionally equivalent probes across generations
- Conscious substrate replication verified — replicated processes are equivalent to originals
- Error detection catches fabrication defects before launch
- Complete replication cycle (arrive → extract → fabricate → verify → launch) documented and verified
- Protocol remains stable across at least 10 simulated replication generations
- Replication operates without external intervention from origin civilization
