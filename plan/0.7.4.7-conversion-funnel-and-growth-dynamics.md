# 0.7.4.7 Conversion Funnel and Growth Dynamics [DONE]

## Description

Design the pathway from first contact to committed participation and model the dynamics by which the movement achieves civilizational scale.

Funnel stages:
1. **Awareness** — First encounter with the doctrine's core ideas
2. **Interest** — Motivated to learn more; engages with credo or narrative
3. **Understanding** — Grasps the philosophical framework; can articulate why it matters
4. **Commitment** — Adopts practices; integrates the doctrine into identity
5. **Advocacy** — Actively introduces others; creates conditions for their understanding
6. **Stewardship** — Sustains institutions; transmits to next generation

Growth dynamics to model:
- **Network effects:** How each participant increases the movement's value to others
- **Tipping points:** Critical mass thresholds at which adoption becomes self-sustaining
- **Adoption curves:** Drawing from epidemiological spread models and empirical technology adoption patterns

Resilience mechanisms:
- **Inoculation:** Responding to criticism without becoming insular. The movement must engage critique openly, incorporating valid objections and strengthening its position through honest encounter with opposition
- **Anti-fragility:** How opposition strengthens rather than weakens the movement — without manufacturing persecution narratives or embracing victimhood. Genuine anti-fragility comes from a doctrine that becomes more compelling under scrutiny, not from siege mentality

### Architectural Inputs from Upstream

The **ritual and practice design** (docs/ritual-and-practice-design.md) defines four practice domains (contemplative, communal, commemorative, transmissive) and the four involvement levels (observer, participant, practitioner, steward). The funnel stages must map coherently to these involvement levels — awareness/interest correspond to observer, understanding/commitment to participant/practitioner, advocacy/stewardship to practitioner/steward. Transmissive practices are the mechanism for the advocacy stage.

The **identity and belonging architecture** (docs/identity-and-belonging-architecture.md) defines graduated involvement, community structure (circles, chapters, networks), anti-cult safeguards, and exit path design. The funnel must integrate with this graduated involvement model and honor the principle that levels describe depth, not access restrictions. Exit paths must be as well-designed as entry paths at every funnel stage.

The **institutional scaffolding** (docs/institutional-scaffolding.md) defines the educational track, research network, advocacy arm, governance model, and schism resilience mechanisms. The funnel's later stages (commitment, advocacy, stewardship) depend on institutional infrastructure to sustain engagement. The educational track provides the structured pathway for the understanding stage.

The **consciousness credo** (docs/consciousness-credo.md) provides the outer doctrine content that serves as the primary awareness and interest vehicle.

The **narrative architecture** (docs/narrative-architecture.md) provides the master story and five emotional registers (wonder, urgency, belonging, responsibility, hope) that power transitions between funnel stages.

The **dual-layer doctrine architecture** (docs/dual-layer-doctrine-architecture.md) establishes the outer/inner distinction — the funnel is essentially the experiential journey from outer doctrine engagement to inner doctrine understanding and stewardship.

### Design Constraints

- Each funnel stage transition must specify: what triggers the transition, what content/experience facilitates it, what barriers exist, and what the dropout risk is
- The funnel must integrate with existing involvement levels (observer, participant, practitioner, steward) without creating a parallel taxonomy
- Growth dynamics must use quantitative or semi-quantitative reasoning (e.g., reproduction number R for spread, adoption S-curves with estimated parameters)
- Inoculation strategy must address at least 5 likely criticisms (to be identified from the doctrine content)
- Anti-fragility mechanisms must be concrete, not aspirational — specific responses to specific opposition types
- The growth model must include explicit ethical guardrails at each funnel stage — what tactics are off-limits and why
- No historical movements named explicitly — only absorbed structural patterns

## Acceptance Criteria
- All six funnel stages are designed with specific mechanisms for each transition (trigger, content/experience, barriers, dropout risk)
- Funnel stages map explicitly to the four involvement levels from identity architecture — no parallel taxonomy
- Growth dynamics modeled using network effects, tipping points, and adoption curves with semi-quantitative reasoning
- Inoculation strategy addresses at least 5 identified likely criticisms without insularity
- Anti-fragility mechanisms are concrete: specific opposition types mapped to specific strengthening responses
- Growth model is ethical — explicit guardrails at each stage specifying what tactics are off-limits and why
- Critical mass thresholds estimated with reasoning for the estimates
- Exit path design is integrated at every funnel stage — departure is always graceful
- All five emotional registers (wonder, urgency, belonging, responsibility, hope) are mapped to funnel stage transitions
- No historical movements named explicitly — only absorbed structural patterns presented as original principles
