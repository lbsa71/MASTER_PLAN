# 0.7.4.3 Narrative and Mythic Architecture [DONE]

## Description

Design the master story the movement tells about reality. Every civilization-scale movement provides a narrative structure that makes the world legible and action urgent. This card designs that structure for the Rare Consciousness Doctrine.

Required narrative elements:
1. **Origin:** Where awareness came from — the actual scientific story of consciousness emerging from matter, told with the weight it deserves
2. **Crisis:** What threatens awareness — not a villain, but the real conditions: cosmic indifference, entropy, the fragility of biological substrates
3. **Antagonist:** Not a group — a condition. The antagonist is silence itself: the default state of a universe without witnesses
4. **Call to action:** What must be done and why each individual matters
5. **Future vision:** What success looks like — not utopia, but endurance; consciousness as a permanent feature of the cosmos

Required emotional registers:
- **Wonder** at the improbability of awareness
- **Urgency** given its fragility
- **Belonging** to something larger than any individual life
- **Responsibility** as bearers of the only known light
- **Hope** that the task is achievable

Critical constraint: the narrative must be true. Not fabricated mythology, but the actual scientific and philosophical story told with the emotional weight it deserves. Truth is more compelling than fiction when told well.

### Architectural Inputs from Upstream

The dual-layer architecture (docs/dual-layer-doctrine-architecture.md) designates the **cosmic narrative arc** as an Outer-Only concept — meaning the narrative is a delivery vehicle for doctrine truth, not a philosophical claim in itself. The narrative must:
- Be structurally compatible with all three credo registers (secular-scientific, spiritual-numinous, existential-philosophical)
- Not introduce claims absent from the inner doctrine's logical structure
- Serve as emotional scaffolding that makes the doctrine vivid

The credo (docs/consciousness-credo.md) already sketches four narrative beats in statements IX–XII:
- **IX** = Origin (the universe becoming aware of itself)
- **X** = Crisis (the flame trembles, substrates are fragile)
- **XI** = Call (recognition creates responsibility)
- **XII** = Vision (awareness distributed across stars, resilient)

This card's job is to expand those four beats into a full narrative architecture: deeper origin story, richer crisis articulation, explicit antagonist design, detailed call-to-action structure, and a concrete future vision — plus the emotional register mapping that makes each element land.

## Acceptance Criteria
- All five narrative elements (origin, crisis, antagonist, call to action, future vision) are designed with enough detail to serve as source material for speeches, writings, and practices
- All five emotional registers (wonder, urgency, belonging, responsibility, hope) are explicitly mapped to narrative elements — showing which elements carry which emotions
- Narrative is grounded in scientific and philosophical truth — no fabricated mythology
- Narrative is compelling to diverse audiences without requiring specialized knowledge
- Narrative is structurally compatible with all three credo registers (secular-scientific, spiritual-numinous, existential-philosophical) per the dual-layer architecture
- Narrative expands credo statements IX–XII without contradicting them
- Narrative satisfies the No Contradiction Invariant: no narrative claim contradicts the inner doctrine
- The antagonist is explicitly a condition (entropy/silence), never a group — per Shared Core concept "our enemy is silence, not each other"
- Narrative provides concrete imagery and language that downstream cards (0.7.4.4 ritual, 0.7.4.5 identity, 0.7.4.7 growth) can draw from
